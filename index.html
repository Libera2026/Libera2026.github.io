About the Workshop

This workshop aims to bring together leading researchers from Europe and the Americas working on stochastic processes and their dynamics, stochastic control and optimization with applications across mathematics, physics, statistics, and machine learning. We would be honored if you could join us to present your recent work and share your insights with the community. This event is partially supported by the Marie-Curie network LiBERA. This midterm meeting tracks progress and fosters new collaborations.

Scientific Scope

Stochastic differential equations, mean-field games, optimal transport, reinforcement learning, stochastic control, and applications in finance, economics, and engineering.

The LiBERA Network

LiBERA is a Marie Skłodowska-Curie research network bringing together European mathematicians to advance the theory of stochastic interacting systems.

Who Should Attend

Mathematicians, engineers, and computational scientists working on stochastic systems — from PhD students to senior researchers — are warmly invited to participate.

Venue

Venue

Bogotá is the vibrant capital of Colombia, situated on a high-altitude savanna at 2,600 metres above sea level in the heart of the Andes. A city of over eight million people, it blends colonial history with a thriving contemporary culture, world-class gastronomy, and a dynamic academic scene.

Getting There

Bogotá is served by El Dorado International Airport (BOG), one of the busiest in Latin America, with direct connections to major cities in Europe, North America, and across South America.

Climate

At 2,600 m altitude, Bogotá has a mild, spring-like climate year-round with temperatures between 7°C and 19°C. July is typically dry and sunny — ideal for the workshop. A light jacket is recommended for evenings.

Getting Around

The city has an extensive bus rapid transit system (TransMilenio) and a growing metro line. Taxis and ride-hailing apps (InDriver, Uber) are widely available and affordable.

Things to Do

Explore the historic La Candelaria neighbourhood, visit the Gold Museum (Museo del Oro), stroll through Usaquén, or take a cable car up to Monserrate for panoramic views of the city.

Accommodation

A range of hotels is available near the workshop venue. We recommend staying in the Chapinero, Zona Rosa, or Usaquén neighbourhoods for easy access to restaurants and transport. Details to be announced.

Visa & Entry

Citizens of most countries can enter Colombia visa-free for up to 90 days. Please check the requirements for your nationality with the Colombian consulate or embassy in advance.
