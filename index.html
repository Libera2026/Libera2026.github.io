About the Workshop

This workshop aims to bring together leading researchers from Europe and the Americas working on stochastic processes and their dynamics, stochastic control and optimization with applications across mathematics, physics, statistics, and machine learning. We would be honored if you could join us to present your recent work and share your insights with the community. This event is partially supported by the Marie-Curie network LiBERA. This midterm meeting tracks progress and fosters new collaborations.

Scientific Scope

Stochastic differential equations, mean-field games, optimal transport, reinforcement learning, stochastic control, and applications in finance, economics, and engineering.

The LiBERA Network

LiBERA is a Marie Skłodowska-Curie research network bringing together European mathematicians to advance the theory of stochastic interacting systems.

Who Should Attend

Mathematicians, engineers, and computational scientists working on stochastic systems — from PhD students to senior researchers — are warmly invited to participate.

Venue

The workshop will be held at Universidad de los Andes, a leading Colombian university in Bogotá, a vibrant academic hub at 2,600 m altitude in the heart of South America. Venue details to be announced.

About Bogotá

Bogotá is a city of over eight million people, it blends colonial history with a thriving contemporary culture, world-class gastronomy, and a dynamic academic scene.

Getting There
El Dorado International Airport (BOG) is one of the busiest in Latin America, with direct connections to major cities in Europe, North America, and across South America.
Climate

At 2,600 m altitude, Bogotá has a mild, spring-like climate year-round with temperatures between 7°C and 19°C. July is typically dry and sunny — ideal for the workshop.

A light/rain jacket is recommended for evenings but also since in Bogotá, there is always a nonzero probability of rain at any given time.

Getting Around
The city has an extensive bus rapid transit system (TransMilenio). Taxis and ride-hailing apps (Uber and Didi) are widely available and affordable.
Things to Do
Explore the historic La Candelaria neighbourhood, visit the Gold Museum (Museo del Oro), stroll through Usaquén, or take a cable car up to Monserrate for panoramic views of the city.
